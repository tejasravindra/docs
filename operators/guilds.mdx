## What are Guilds?

Guilds are **cross-functional teams of 2-4 operators** assembled for projects that require multiple skill sets or accelerated timelines. They represent Startery's approach to collaborative excellence.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Special Ops Approach" icon="users-gear">
    Small, elite teams with complementary skills working in tight coordination.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cross-Functional" icon="arrows-cross">
    Multiple disciplines (strategy, design, engineering, operations) working together.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Project-Based" icon="project-diagram">
    Assembled for specific initiatives, dissolved upon completion.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Elite Quality" icon="star">
    Only experienced operators (typically Associate level\+) participate.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

  <Tab title="Product + Operations">

    - **Product Manager**: Strategy, roadmapping, user research
    - **Operations Specialist**: Process design, automation, metrics

    - Customer success system design
    - Business process optimization

## Guild Formation Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Project Analysis">
    Scoping Agent identifies projects requiring multiple skill sets or accelerated timelines.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Skill Mapping">
    Staffing Agent analyzes required competencies and identifies optimal operator combinations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Team Assembly">
    Operators are invited based on availability, skill fit, and collaboration history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Kickoff">
    Guild members meet for project alignment, role clarification, and workflow establishment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collaborative Execution">
    Regular check-ins, integrated workflows, and shared accountability for outcomes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Working in Guilds

### Collaboration Best Practices

- **Daily async updates** in shared channels
- **Weekly sync meetings** for alignment and problem-solving
- **Shared documentation** for transparency and knowledge transfer
- **Integrated workflows** using compatible tools and processes
- **Cross-discipline feedback** to improve collective output

### Role Clarity

While Guilds work collaboratively, each member maintains:

- **Primary expertise** in their domain
- **Shared responsibility** for overall project success
- **Individual accountability** for their deliverables
- **Collective ownership** of the final outcome

## Joining Guilds

### Eligibility Requirements

- **Associate level or above** (demonstrated client-readiness)
- **Proven track record** of successful project delivery
- **Strong collaboration skills** and team mindset
- **Complementary expertise** to potential Guild compositions

### Getting Invited

- **Consistent excellence** in individual projects
- **Positive feedback** from clients and colleagues
- **Availability** for multi-week collaborative commitments
- **Skill combinations** that match common Guild needs
